0:20 – 0:45 · the problem

SayAn agent that does nothing costs little. But the moment you make it useful — you give it the instructions, you make it hold the context of the call — on a long conversation two things happen at once: the cost explodes, and it loses the thread anyway. OpenAI says so: coherence degrades as the context grows. Real calls last ten minutes, not two. That's why you don't see them in production yet.

SayA solution exists today, but it's hand-made: every team rewrites it from scratch. We packaged it. A layer that anyone building a voice agent adds to their app — and it just works.

1:00 – 2:00 · the demo, the heart
DoShow the two columns at minute ten. Point, one at a time: the left that forgets, the right that remembers, the memory writing itself on the side, the cost pulling apart.

SaySame agent, same voice, same call. On the left, without our layer: it forgets. On the right, with Whisperer: it remembers everything — the watch, the graduation, before the 20th, the delivery to signora Pina.

How? A small model distills the call into a living summary — this one here — and the agent carries that, not the whole conversation. It remembers minute one without dragging ten minutes of context behind it, which is what makes the cost explode the normal way.

And we measured the number: without the layer, zero times out of ten; with the layer, ten out of ten. With a citation for every verdict. Not by gut feel.

What wins it

The opening with the grandmother's real voice — loud and clean.

The line: “zero out of ten, ten out of ten, measured, with a citation”. That's the one that convinces.

The why-us: a problem we've lived, users already waiting, and "in production Monday".

Don't get this wrong

StudierAI is Giovanni's company, not yours. Don't claim you co-founded it — say "my teammate Giovanni co-founded it". You built the layer; they ship it. In front of an investor, precision is credibility.

The 0-out-of-10 and 10-out-of-10 number is measured, real. On cost, don't say "we cost less than the base" (a base that forgets costs less): say that remembering the normal way, holding all the context, makes the cost explode, and we do it at a fraction. Real figure to cite: StudierAI, €4.50 against €0.13.

Don't mention the AI Act. It's not needed and it exposes us.

Q&A

"Pipecat already has it"
Yes — inside their framework, off by default and usable only there. That means the need is real. We're the piece you add to Vapi, Retell, Bland or the direct API, where today there's nothing.
"Mem0 already exists"
Mem0 remembers the user across calls. We hold the thread within the same long call. That's the gap left uncovered.
"But doesn't adding a model cost more?"
Yes, a little more than an agent that truncates and forgets — but that one forgets, it's not a product. The right comparison is with the only other way to remember: keeping the whole conversation in context. And there the cost explodes. We give the same memory at a fraction of that cost. It's not free: it's the only way to remember without blowing up the bill.
"The new 128k window solves everything"
No. OpenAI itself says coherence drops as the context grows, truncating old messages stays blind, and the whole context is re-paid every turn. A bigger window also means a bigger cost.
"How did you measure the number?"
An automatic judge: same transcript, same recall question, ten runs per side, a yes-or-no answer with the turn citation. It's repeatable and always gives the same result.
